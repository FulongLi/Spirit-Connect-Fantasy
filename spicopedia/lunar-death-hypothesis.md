    <h1>Lunar Death Hypothesis</h1>
    <p class="lead">
      The Lunar Death Hypothesis — sometimes called "dead sphere theory" — proposes that where a person dies
      determines where their consciousness can go.
    </p>
  </div>
</header>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Overview</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      In its strongest form, the hypothesis claims that consciousness released at death on Earth is bound by
      the planet's magnetic, ionospheric, or deeper informational potential well — recycled, dispersed, or
      re-bound. Death on a "dead sphere" such as the Moon, the argument goes, may allow consciousness to
      bypass that well entirely and enter a higher informational field.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Status and warning</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      The hypothesis is attributed to a small circle of consciousness physicists and has never been published
      in a reviewed venue. It is unverifiable by definition, and it is dangerous in the most literal sense:
      it gives a reason to die in a particular place. No certified practitioner will discuss it as a procedure.
      It is documented here as an idea, not an option.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>
