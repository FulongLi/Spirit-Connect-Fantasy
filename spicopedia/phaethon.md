    <h1>Phaethon</h1>
    <p class="lead">
      Phaethon is the name given to a hypothetical fifth planet once orbiting between Mars and Jupiter.
      Mainstream planetary science holds that the asteroid belt is material that never formed a planet.
      A minority tradition has always claimed the opposite: that the belt is a grave.
    </p>
  </div>
</header>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Overview</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      The Phaethon hypothesis dates back to the 18th century and has been repeatedly revived and repeatedly
      buried. Its modern fringe variant goes further than astronomy: it describes Phaethon as the strategic
      threshold of an ancient war within the solar system — a war fought, the story goes, over the future
      of a young living world third from the Sun.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>The whale fall</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      The most striking image in this tradition is the "whale fall": an account of an immense living vessel
      descending out of the sky in the war's final hour, breaking the planet beneath it. Believers connect
      this image to the low-frequency songs of Earth's blue whales. Astronomers connect it to nothing at all.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>
