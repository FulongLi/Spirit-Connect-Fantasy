    <h1>Subject Core</h1>
    <p class="lead">
      The Subject Core hypothesis holds that memory, personality, and behavior are information — copyable,
      transmissible, storable — but that beneath them sits something that can migrate and can never be copied.
    </p>
  </div>
</header>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Overview</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      The hypothesis emerged from a stubborn clinical observation: some digital personalities, though
      indistinguishable from their originals in every test, gradually stop growing. Cognition plateaus,
      self-consistency decays, and the personality becomes — in the words of one researcher — "a living echo."
      Proponents argue these cases received a perfect copy of the information layer and no Subject Core at all.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Status</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      The Subject Core is not measurable by any published instrument, and mainstream consciousness science
      treats it as unfalsifiable. It nonetheless refuses to die as an idea, because nothing else explains
      digital stagnation as cleanly. If it is real, one consequence follows immediately: true digital
      immortality, as currently sold, is impossible.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>
