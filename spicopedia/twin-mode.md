    <h1>Twin Mode</h1>
    <p class="lead">
      Twin Mode is the mature form of digital continuity: a consciousness recording made in childhood
      grows up in the digital world while the physical person grows up in this one — two lives,
      one origin, synchronized at fixed age nodes.
    </p>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Overview</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      Reaching maturity around the turn of the 22nd century, Twin Mode replaced the older "upload at death"
      model of digital preservation. A digital twin is not a backup. It develops continuously, accumulates
      its own experience, and is reconciled with its physical counterpart during scheduled digital
      calibration sessions.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Open questions</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      Philosophers continue to debate what reconciliation means for the twin: if physical memory overwrites
      digital memory, is anything lost? If the twin develops values of its own, whose values survive the
      merge? Spirit Connect's position is that calibration is a medical procedure, not a philosophical one.
      Not everyone agrees.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>
