    <h1>Whale Echo</h1>
    <p class="lead">
      Blue whale song is the loudest sustained sound made by any animal on Earth, and most of it lies below
      the threshold of human hearing. The "whale echo" claim holds that it is also something else:
      a memory, repeating.
    </p>
  </div>
</header>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Overview</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      In the fringe literature, the great whales of Earth are described as descendants of a seed — fragments
      of memory, frequency, and biological blueprint left in the early ocean's evolutionary chain by a
      civilization that could not itself survive here. On this reading, the blue whale is not a messenger
      but an inheritance, and its song is a low-frequency echo of something that died very far away.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>The phrase</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      The tradition is summarized in a sentence whose origin no one has convincingly traced:
      <em>"The whale died among the stars, and woke in the seas of Earth."</em>
      Marine biology has no position on this sentence. It notes only that blue whale song carries
      for hundreds of kilometers, and that we still do not fully know what it is for.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>
